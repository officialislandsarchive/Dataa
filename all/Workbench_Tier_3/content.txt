The workbench tier 3 is a crafting station used to craft some of the most advanced items . It has many more crafting recipes than its predecessor, the workbench tier 2 .

Upgrading [ ]

The workbench tier 2 can be upgraded into a workbench tier 3 with 400 iron ingots , 50 stone , and 20 maple wood . Upgrading the crafting station will replace the workbench tier 2 with a workbench tier 3.

Upgrading [ ]

The workbench tier 3 can be upgraded into a workbench tier 4 with 250 red bronze ingots and 250 hickory wood . Upgrading the crafting station will replace the workbench tier 3 with a workbench tier 4.

History [ ]

April 10, 2020 Added workbench tier 3s. September 18, 2020 Changed the recipe of workbench tier 3s from 20 steel rods , 50 stone bricks , and 20 maple wood to 450 iron , 50 stone bricks , and 20 maple wood . July 23, 2021 Changed the recipe of workbench tier 3s from 450 iron , 50 stone bricks , and 20 maple wood to 400 iron , 50 stone , and 20 maple wood .

Trivia [ ]

Before the May 10, 2020 update, conveyors were crafted in this crafting station instead of in anvils . On September 18, 2020 , new players could not craft the workbench tier 3 because its recipe required steel rods , an item that was not possible to craft with the recipes in the workbench tier 2 due to the introduction of the steel mill . This was fixed the following day.
